## DECK O1 — PHYSICS (getting up and staying up)

### O1-C1 COUNTDOWN
FACT: Konstantin Tsiolkovsky published the rocket equation in 1903:
delta-v = ve * ln(m0/m1). Every gram of payload is paid for in propellant;
the logarithm is why rockets are almost entirely fuel.
ON THE RIG: Lines (vertical streaks) pulse on bass. Bed: blank leader.
Body: a bent elbow pumping — ignition hold.

### O1-C2 KEPLER
FACT: Johannes Kepler, 1609 (Astronomia Nova) and 1619 (Harmonices Mundi):
orbits are ellipses, equal areas in equal times, T^2 proportional to a^3.
Planetary motion became geometry.
ON THE RIG: Rings ride the mids; Structure draws the ellipse (Shaper).
Body: mirrored wrists — instruments in agreement.

### O1-C3 HOHMANN
FACT: Walter Hohmann, 1925: the minimum-fuel path between two circular
orbits is a transfer ellipse touched by two burns — one to leave, one to
arrive. Still the workhorse of mission design.
ON THE RIG: Spiral on mids; Structure: rotated lines = the transfer path.
Body: palms braced.

### O1-C4 MAX Q
FACT: Dynamic pressure q = 1/2 * rho * v^2. Max Q is the moment of peak
aerodynamic stress — thrust is often throttled down to survive it.
NOTE (honesty): altitude varies by vehicle (~11-13 km for many launchers),
and in real flight Max Q precedes orbit insertion. Grid order serves the
set's energy contour, not strict flight sequence.
ON THE RIG: Stroboscope gated hard on low bass (floor 0 — true blackouts).
Bed: color flicker. Body: clenched knuckles. Structure: stress-grid checker.

### O1-C5 INSERTION
FACT: Low Earth orbit: about 7.66 km/s, one lap in about 92.9 minutes.
Newton's cannonball (Principia, 1687): orbit is falling and missing the
ground forever.
ON THE RIG: Tunnelines on bass; Structure closes the circle (Shaper).
Body: neck muscles under g-load.

### O1-C6 FREE FALL
FACT: Weightlessness is continuous fall. Einstein called the 1907 insight —
a falling person feels no weight — the happiest thought of his life; it
led to general relativity.
ON THE RIG: Metaballs drift on slow bass (1800 ms fallback). Structure dark
by design: free fall has nothing to hold. Body: lips, a breath.

### O1-C7 RESONANCE
FACT: Io, Europa and Ganymede are locked 1:2:4 (the Laplace resonance).
Kirkwood gaps (1866): resonances with Jupiter sweep the asteroid belt clean
at specific periods.
ON THE RIG: Geometry Pattern Maker on mids; Structure: rings at 1:2:4.

### O1-C8 ESCAPE
FACT: Escape velocity from Earth's surface is 11.186 km/s — an idealized
value that ignores atmosphere and rotation.
NOTE (honesty): gravity has no cutoff; the well just widens forever. Escape
means your orbit never closes, not that you leave gravity behind.
ON THE RIG: Line Scape on highs; Structure dark by design — past structure.
Body: a single fingertip.

### O1-C9 TRANSIT: CRUISE

## DECK O3 — STELLAR (stars as machines)

### O3-C1 FUSION
FACT: The proton-proton chain converts hydrogen to helium; 0.7% of the mass
becomes light. A photon random-walks roughly a hundred thousand years from
the core to the surface, then reaches Earth in eight minutes.
ON THE RIG: Metaballs merge on slow bass — nucleons finding each other.
Bed: star-birth composite.
NOTE (curation): C1 bed clip carries a baked-in caption + watermark —
P2 swap candidate, curator's call.

### O3-C2 SUNSPOTS
FACT: Heinrich Schwabe, 1843: sunspot counts cycle over about 11 years.
The Sun's magnetic field flips polarity each cycle — the full magnetic
period is about 22 years.
ON THE RIG: Golden Flicker Reel on mids; Structure: slow circular pan —
sunspot rotation.

### O3-C3 SOLAR WIND
FACT: Eugene Parker predicted a continuous supersonic solar wind in 1958;
spacecraft confirmed it. 400-700 km/s at Earth. The aurora is its visible
detector — oxygen's green line at 557.7 nm.
ON THE RIG: Sine Wave on highs; Structure: steady wind (Parker steady take).

### O3-C4 SUPERNOVA
FACT: SN 1054 was recorded by Chinese astronomers; its debris is the Crab
Nebula. Core collapse disperses newly forged elements into the galaxy.
NOTE (honesty): the heaviest elements come mostly from neutron-star
mergers — GW170817 (2017) made that measurable.
ON THE RIG: Stroboscope gated on low bass, floor 0. Bed: paint-swirl burst.
Structure: rapidfire montage.

### O3-C5 PULSAR — THE LIGHTHOUSE IN THE NOISE
FACT: In 1967 Jocelyn Bell Burnell, then a graduate student, noticed a
"bit of scruff" recurring in 400 feet of chart paper from the Cambridge
radio array. The source, PSR B1919+21, pulsed every 1.3373 seconds —
too regular for anything then known. It was briefly labeled LGM-1. It is
a neutron star: a stellar core heavier than the Sun, twenty kilometers
wide, spinning, sweeping a radio beam past Earth like a lighthouse.
The 1974 Nobel Prize went to her supervisor; the record has been argued
about ever since. The Crab pulsar spins at 33 ms; the fastest known,
PSR J1748-2446ad, at 716 Hz — a city-sized object spinning faster than
a kitchen blender.
NOTE (sonification honesty): pulsar pulses are radio intensity, not sound.
Every pulsar "sound" you have heard is a sonification — a chosen mapping
of radio flux to audio. The famous stacked-ridgeline plot of B1919+21
(Harold Craft's 1970 thesis, later the cover of Unknown Pleasures) is a
chart, not a waveform. This scene's ReactPulsar plugin does the same
thing honestly in reverse: your music's bass drives the Level of a
CP1919-style ridgeline stack. It is a mapping. It never claims to be
the star.
ON THE RIG: ReactPulsar (FFGL source, built and shipped this session) —
Level rides the bass; stack sparse (N~33) so the layer Bloom does not
wash the ridgelines white. Structure: the dish stares (surveillance take).

### O3-C6 ACCRETION
FACT: The Event Horizon Telescope imaged M87* in 2019 and Sagittarius A*
in 2022 — the shadow and the glowing accretion flow. Inside the innermost
stable circular orbit, matter spirals in with no stable path left.
ON THE RIG: Spiral tightening on bass; Structure: smooth crane orbit.

### O3-C7 DISPERSION
FACT: Interstellar plasma delays radio waves by DM * f^-2 — low
frequencies arrive LATE. B1919+21's dispersion measure is 12.44. The
smear in arrival times is how we weigh the electrons between us and
the star.
ON THE RIG: Tunnelines on mids; Structure: multichannel sequence — the
same signal arriving staggered.

### O3-C8 HR DIAGRAM
FACT: Hertzsprung and Russell, 1911-1913: plot luminosity against
temperature and every star's life appears on one quiet chart. Lifetime
scales roughly as M^-2.5 — the brightest stars die youngest.
ON THE RIG: Geometry Pattern Maker whispering on highs (+3 dB). Bed dark
by design — the quiet plot carries the cell.

### O3-C9 TRANSIT: INTERSTELLAR
FACT: The Solar System is crossing the Local Bubble — a cavity about

live inside an old explosion.
ON THE RIG: Abstract Field on slow bass. Bed intentionally dark tonight:

## DECK O2 — PLANETS (Solar System physics)

### O2-C1 MERCURY
FACT: Mercury is locked in a 3:2 spin-orbit resonance — three rotations
every two orbits. Its perihelion precesses 43 arcseconds per century more
than Newton allows; explaining that anomaly was general relativity's first
triumph (1915).
ON THE RIG: Golden Flicker on highs; volcanic heat bed; polished slab.

### O2-C2 VENUS
FACT: A Venusian day (243 Earth days) outlasts its year (225), and it
rotates backwards. 92 bar of CO2, 464 C — the runaway greenhouse, hotter
than Mercury despite being farther out.
ON THE RIG: Metaballs on slow bass; acid-murk bed; dim thick interior.

### O2-C3 EARTH-MOON
FACT: Apollo crews left retroreflectors on the Moon; laser ranging shows
it receding 3.8 cm per year — tidal energy traded for orbital distance.
The tides in the sea and the slowing of the day are the same transaction.
ON THE RIG: Sine Wave on mids (tidal curves); ocean bed; the tide river.

### O2-C4 MARS
FACT: Global dust storms can swallow the planet for months. Olympus Mons
stands 21.9 km — two and a half Everests. Dust devils leave visible tracks
photographed from orbit.
ON THE RIG: Stroboscope gated on low bass, floor 0 (dust-devil strikes);
metal desert bed; colossal massif.

### O2-C5 JUPITER
FACT: The Great Red Spot has been tracked for over 190 years. A 10-hour
day flattens the planet visibly; the storm is wider than Earth.
ON THE RIG: Spiral on bass; storm-lake bed; the majestic circular chamber.

### O2-C6 SATURN
FACT: The rings span ~280,000 km but are locally only ~10 meters thick.
Inside the Roche limit (~2.44 radii) moons cannot hold together — the
rings are what tides forbid from becoming a moon. The Cassini gap is a
2:1 resonance with Mimas.
ON THE RIG: Rings on mids; carousel bed; identical translucent stones.

### O2-C7 URANUS
FACT: Tilted 97.77 degrees — it rolls around its orbit, giving 42-year
seasons. William Herschel found it in 1781: the first planet discovered
with a telescope.
ON THE RIG: Lines on highs (tilt via Transform = polish item); cold
sideways ocean bed; silent black corridor.

### O2-C8 NEPTUNE
FACT: Winds near 2,100 km/h — the fastest in the Solar System, on the
planet farthest from the Sun. Le Verrier computed its position from
Uranus's misbehavior in 1846; it was found within a degree. A planet
discovered by arithmetic.
ON THE RIG: Line Scape on mids; retro deep-blue bed; the vast underground.

### O2-C9 TRANSIT: HELIOPAUSE
FACT: The heliopause is where the solar wind stalls against the
interstellar medium — NOT the Solar System's gravitational edge (the Oort
cloud reaches a thousand times farther). Voyager 1 crossed it in 2012 at
122 AU and is still calling home.
ON THE RIG: Abstract Field on highs +9; the explorer leaves; serene
cosmic surface. Body: the console keeps watching (Paik, 1984).

## DECK O4 — GALACTIC

### O4-C1 GALACTIC YEAR
FACT: The Sun orbits the galactic center at about 230 km/s, once every
~230 million years. The last time we were here, dinosaurs were new.
ON THE RIG: Rings breathing on slow bass; the great mandala wheel.

### O4-C2 DENSITY WAVES
FACT: Lin and Shu, 1964: spiral arms are traffic jams, not structures —
stars drift through them; the pattern persists while the members change.
ON THE RIG: Spiral on mids; kaleidoscope journey bed; sphere meets
flatland.

### O4-C3 ROTATION CURVE
FACT: Vera Rubin and Kent Ford, 1970: galaxy rotation curves stay FLAT
far beyond the visible disk. Either most matter is invisible, or gravity
is wrong at scale. Dark matter in one line on a chart.
ON THE RIG: Lines on bass — the plateau, literally. Body: the dish cranes.

### O4-C4 SGR A*
FACT: 4.15 million solar masses at the Milky Way's center. The star S2
swings past at 7,650 km/s — measured by the GRAVITY interferometer (2018,
Nobel 2020). The bed is black on purpose.
ON THE RIG: Tight Metaballs orbiting on bass; high-speed tracking body.

### O4-C5 LENSING
FACT: Eddington's 1919 eclipse measured starlight bending 1.75 arcseconds
around the Sun. Einstein rings and the 1979 twin quasar are the same
physics scaled up: mass is a lens.
ON THE RIG: Sine Wave on bass; mandala-burst bed = the ReactLens carrier
when the plugin ships (P2/P3). Body: Eddington's plate close-up.

### O4-C6 COLLISION
FACT: The Antennae galaxies are mid-merger; the Milky Way and Andromeda
meet in ~4.5 billion years. Galaxies collide; their stars almost never
do — the gaps are that large.
ON THE RIG: Geometry Pattern Maker on mids; fractal interpenetration bed;

### O4-C7 GLOBULARS

### O4-C8 LOCAL GROUP

## DECK O5 — COSMIC (the tab is near-black by design; the void is the subject)

### O5-C1 REDSHIFT
FACT: z = delta-lambda / lambda. Hubble, 1929: distance correlates with
redshift. Today's tension: the early-universe route gives H0 ~ 67, the
local-ladder route ~73, and the disagreement has survived a decade of
better data.
ON THE RIG: Sine Wave drifting on slow mids; the long drift out
(Cybercathedral to Void).

### O5-C2 CMB
FACT: 2.7255 K in every direction — light from 380,000 years after the
Big Bang (Penzias and Wilson, 1965, via a persistent antenna hiss).
Roughly 1% of old analog-TV snow was this signal.
ON THE RIG: Abstract Field shimmer on highs; faint uniform glow bed;
anisotropy texture on Structure.

### O5-C3 COSMIC WEB
FACT: Galaxies arrange along filaments around voids. Baryon acoustic
oscillations froze a ~490-million-light-year ruler into the pattern —
a fossil sound wave used to survey the universe.
ON THE RIG: Lines on mids; the grid symphony carries the web; bed dark.

### O5-C4 INFLATION
FACT: In the first ~10^-32 seconds, space expanded faster than light can
cross it, stretching quantum fluctuations into the seeds of galaxies.
The theory's fingerprints are in the CMB's spot pattern.
ON THE RIG: Stroboscope on low bass, floor 0. Nothing else. It was fast.

### O5-C5 CHIRP
FACT: GW150914, 14 September 2015: two black holes, ~1.3 billion light
years away, heard as a rising chirp. LIGO measured arm-length changes of
about a thousandth of a proton's width. Gravitational astronomy began
as a sound.
ON THE RIG: Rings gated hard on bass (100 ms fallback — the chirp);
black behind.

### O5-C6 DARK ENERGY
FACT: 1998, two supernova teams: the expansion is ACCELERATING. Lambda

The largest thing there is, discovered by dimming embers.
ON THE RIG: Metaballs on slow bass; accelerating void bed; stretching
lattice.

### O5-C7 DEEP FIELD
FACT: December 1995: Hubble stared at an empty patch of Ursa Major for

hours. There is no empty sky.
ON THE RIG: Golden Flicker on highs, gentle (+3); bed deliberately dark —

### O5-C8 NULL FIELD
FACT: Every element heavier than lithium was forged: Big Bang (H, He,
Li), stellar fusion (through iron), supernovae, and neutron-star mergers

### O5-C9 TRANSIT: PALE BLUE DOT
